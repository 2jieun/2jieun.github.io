Inmate’s drinking level as recorded. Hover over squares for specific drinking level description and prisoner count having that label.

Drinking level was a component utilized in describing inmates personal characteristics. According to Janofsky (2004), in the early 1830s, the penitentiary’s physician Dr. Franklin Bache, “reported that three-quarters of the inmate population were incarcerated because of alcohol-related crimes.” We have not been able to determine how influential drinking level alone was in determining inmates’ potential for reformation after committing a crime and landing in Eastern State Penitentiary. We note that drinking level was described in both past and present tense which may be a reflection of perceived reformation. Both varying and distinct terms were utilized in an attempt to accurately define a prisoner’s habits regarding alcohol consumption.
